Banker’s wealth of memories

Author Draws on Boyhood in New Book

“He’d go along the tunnel for six kilometres and pop up at the racetrack,’’ Verlander said. “I was never brave enough to go that far but I had many escapades in the tunnels .’’ Verlander grew up in Williamstown, Melbourne, and has drawn on his memories of boyhood and Williamstown in the 1960s for the setting of his new book, Of White and Shady. The central character is a young man called Michel White who inherits a ballet shoe factory from his grandfather but the windfall brings him into contact with a dubious character called Shady Green. “The book is about the tantalising prospect of wealth and that being snatched away – ultimately it’s about a mystery solved,’’ said Verlander, who lives with his family at Neutral Bay. A banking executive, Verlander is an inspiration to any aspiring writer. He’s always been a voracious reader but says he could never make the time to write. “The more I read, the more I was convinced that I could write a book that would stand up with the competition,’’ he said. “So I got myself organised and used the weekends to write.” Of White and Shady is available at Pages and Pages Mosman and Lesley McKays Bookshop in Woollahra, and major online book sources.

Now that securitisation caper sounds pretty serious, so Verlander must've been hard pressed to find the time to write the thing. Let's hope it's a bestseller – although to someone accustomed to selling wholesale debt, selling retail books might seem a few too many zeros short.

About Robert

Robert Verlander was born in Williamstown where much of the action unfolds in Of White and Shady. While raised in Melbourne he moved to Sydney in 1986 to pursue a career in Financial Markets. He is married with two sons and lives on Sydney’s Lower North Shore at Neutral Bay. Of White and Shady is his first novel.
