Robert Verlander joins Harrison Young as CBA novelist

There has got to be something in the water at the biggest of the Big Four, the Commonwealth Bank.

Board member Harrison Young was once just a nondescript middle-aged white male in a suit. But now he's a standout, 69-year-old silver fox, and author of erotic fiction. The former NBN chairman's second novel, Submission, hit the stands just before Christmas and reads suspiciously like the symptom of working in close proximity to Stephen Conroy.

And it looks like the bank's management ranks have been inspired by their director's example.

Head of debt markets securitisation Robert Verlander is launching his debut novel, Of White and Shady, next week at Lesley McKay's bookstore in Woollahra. The book is published by Vivid and is set in Melbourne in the late 1960s.

Now that securitisation caper sounds pretty serious, so Verlander must've been hard pressed to find the time to write the thing. Let's hope it's a bestseller – although to someone accustomed to selling wholesale debt, selling retail books might seem a few too many zeros short.

By Joe Aston


Republished from The Australian Financial Review


About Robert

Robert Verlander was born in Williamstown where much of the action unfolds in Of White and Shady. While raised in Melbourne he moved to Sydney in 1986 to pursue a career in Financial Markets. He is married with two sons and lives on Sydney’s Lower North Shore at Neutral Bay. Of White and Shady is his first novel.
